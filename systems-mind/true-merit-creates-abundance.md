Technology generally increases abundance, productivity, knowledge, meaning, and quality of life. Even before AI, humanity already had enough resources and capability to drastically reduce poverty and suffering. The real problem is not technology. It is inefficient human systems that fail to distribute opportunity, dignity, and resources fairly.

I was thinking about the Netflix show “3%.”

If the 3% were truly the most capable people, they should have been able to turn 3% into 300%.

True merit creates abundance for everyone. True merit, innovation and technology only creates more and more abundance and better standards of living for everyone — every life on the planet.

False merit creates inefficiencies that brings in scarcity and confusion and brokenness which are natural byproducts of scarcity.

If the 3% (in the Netflix show) were truly capable, they could have brought abundance to 300%. False merit only gatekeeps and tries to maintain the 3%. But systems built on exclusion eventually decay. If opportunity is concentrated instead of expanded, the 3% eventually collapses toward 0%.

---

## Scarcity Is Often Manufactured

The “permanent underclass” (something that's going viral in social media nowadays) has nothing to do with AI. It existed long before AI because of human inefficiency, lack of empathy, and broken systems. It can continue to exist with AI just like it existed throughout human history. Scarcity is often manufactured. Abundance is natural.

The pie is never fixed. It’s elastic. It can grow from 3% to 300%. And it can shrink too. The pie constantly fluctuates.

The only way to keep the pie enormously humongous, stable, and growing is to build systems rooted in truth, simplicity, competence, and empathy.

---

## What True Merit Means

True merit is not just talking about technology. Not just performing well in interviews. Not just solving LeetCode problems.

True merit has empathy at the center.

True merit means:

* seeing reality for what it is
* refusing to normalize suffering
* refusing to turn a blind eye
* questioning broken assumptions
* understanding the real causes of scarcity
* being honest about failures
* striving to turn 3% into 300%

True merit creates abundance. False merit gatekeeps abundance.

False merit becomes complacent:

* hogging resources
* protecting status
* giving excuses for stagnation
* consuming far more than it creates

It survives temporarily by preserving exclusivity instead of expanding opportunity. But systems built on extraction eventually decay. If companies consume enormous resources but fail to create meaningful value, the pie shrinks.

The money gets used up. And eventually the system collapses.

---

## What Real Innovation Should Expand

Real innovation should expand abundance:

* better lives
* better access
* better opportunity
* better dignity
* less poverty
* less suffering

Technology alone does not guarantee abundance. Truthful, competent, empathetic systems do.

False merit protects broken systems instead of fixing them. False merit criminalizes human struggle while refusing to address the real causes of instability, poverty, and inefficiency.

False merit can punish an unemployed woman for not being able to move out of their extramarital home after divorce, while ignoring the economic and systemic failures that created the situation in the first place.

False merit clings to broken employment systems as if human life and dignity cannot exist outside endless dependency, gatekeeping, and bureaucracy.

People spend years trapped in cycles of:

* job applications
* meaningless interviews
* artificial filtering systems
* endless uncertainty
* wasted human potential

Not because humanity lacks resources or capability, but because inefficient systems continue to reproduce inefficiency.

False merit refuses to remove waste, fraud, corruption, and unnecessary complexity.

---

## True Merit Refuses to Normalize Suffering

True merit is different.

True merit refuses to normalize suffering. It refuses to sit comfortably inside broken systems.

True merit asks:

* Why are these inefficiencies still here?
* Why is abundance not reaching everyone?
* Why is human potential being wasted?
* Why are dignity and opportunity treated as scarce?

True merit strives to fix systems. To reduce friction. To eliminate waste. To create abundance, prosperity, stability, and opportunity for every life on this planet.

False merit consumes and protects scarcity. True merit creates abundance.

In fact woke mind virus, victim hood etc are only natural byproducts of scarcity.

And scarcity is only a casual effect of false merit. And the very people whom true merit could help and uplift can’t recognize this and they keep suppressing the true merit.

People are in need of true merit to help create abundance and eliminate broken systems and scarcity. But people don’t realize this and they keep suppressing true merit.

## Nobody Asks the Right Questions

Example:

Why do we have job applications wasting job seekers time and inefficient meaningless interviews that makes employment broken?

Nobody asks the right questions.

Nobody wants to fix the broken systems. And bring meaning to human life. And Scarcity is the result of this. Which has many byproducts like confusion, frustration, victimhood, etc…

Why do people who worked and contributed all their life have to be homeless? Why do anyone (even if they didn’t get the opportunity to work and contribute) have to live in poverty and homeless?

Nobody asks the right questions.

Instead people try to pull down true merit as if that’s going to do something good. But in fact it’s only very bad for everyone.

True merit is essential for abundance for everyone because it can only be achieved by true progress, technology and innovation and eliminating fraud, waste and broken meaningless systems that causes inefficiencies.

## When True Merit Is Missing, Understanding Disappears

When there is a lack of true merit, there is a lack of understanding.

People stop asking the right questions. They normalize broken systems. They confuse gatekeeping with competence. They mistake accumulation for progress. They defend inefficiency as if it is necessary.

A lack of true merit creates shallow thinking.

It prevents people from understanding:

* the real causes of scarcity
* the root causes of poverty and suffering
* why systems become inefficient and unstable
* why human potential gets wasted
* why abundance fails to reach everyone

Without true merit, society loses the ability to see clearly. And when understanding disappears, broken systems continue reproducing confusion, waste, inequality, frustration, and suffering generation after generation.

When there is a lack of true merit, there is also a lack of understanding. People stop recognizing what merit is actually for.

Instead of supporting true merit to improve systems and reduce suffering, many try to stand on top of true merit and use it as a ladder to climb, dominate, exploit, or accumulate more power and wealth for themselves.

But only a small number of people can climb that ladder. And in the process, the ladder itself becomes broken. The system becomes unstable, exclusionary, and inefficient.

---

## Poverty Cannot Be Eliminated Through Greed

People need to understand something very simple:

Poverty cannot be eliminated through greed, gatekeeping, or endless competition. Poverty can only be eliminated by fixing broken systems and sharing with empathy. Greed is one of the main causes of poverty.

If there are only 100 hours of work available for 20 people, a healthy and empathetic society should have the heart to divide those hours in a way that allows all 20 people to survive, contribute, and live with dignity.

That is how stable abundance is created.

Not by forcing a few people to overwork while others are discarded. Not by concentrating opportunity into fewer and fewer hands. Not by treating human beings as disposable.

To eliminate poverty and create abundance for everyone, humanity does not primarily need more gatekeeping.

It needs empathy. It needs wisdom.

It needs systems designed around dignity, fairness, sustainability, and shared prosperity. We do not need endless artificial filters, DSA interviews, behavioral performances, bureaucracy, or meaningless competition to create abundance.

We need truthful systems, competent systems, and compassionate systems. Abundance grows when society values human well-being more than greed.
